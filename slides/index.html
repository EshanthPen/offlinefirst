Education without internet.

A learning platform that runs entirely offline and syncs between devices over local WiFi.

offlinefirst By Eshanth Penumatsa
The gap
2.6B

2.6 billion people have no reliable internet access.

300 million of them are school-age children.

Every major edtech platform assumes connectivity. The students who need education most are the ones least able to use it.

OfflineFirst

Lessons cache on each device. Quizzes save locally. Devices share content with each other over local WiFi using WebRTC, even when the internet is gone.

Three layers

How it works without the internet

App shell, cached

A service worker stores every JS, CSS, image. After the first visit, the app loads with zero network requests.

Lessons + scores, local

IndexedDB stores every lesson, every quiz answer. The UI never knows whether the internet exists.

Devices, peer to peer

WebRTC opens direct channels between phones over local WiFi. New lessons travel device to device, no internet hop.

The scenario

A school day with intermittent internet

8:00 AM
Teacher visits café with WiFi. Tablet downloads new lessons. Uploads yesterday's quiz scores.
9:05 AM
Students open the app at school. No internet. The cached app loads. Sample lessons appear instantly.
9:10 AM
Students near the teacher tablet sync over local WiFi. Today's new content arrives in seconds.
9:30 AM
Students take a quiz. Scores save locally. Result screen shows immediately. No spinner.
4:00 PM
Teacher returns to WiFi. Class scores upload to the server. Dashboard updates for parents and admins.
What's built

One app, every role, every language

Student

Read lessons with audio playback. Take quizzes offline. Track progress per subject. Larger-text mode for low vision.

Teacher

Author lessons in a Notion-style editor. Import CSV or JSON. Monitor the live mesh. Export PDF reports.

Universal

Four languages: English, Spanish, French, Swahili. Light and dark themes. Installs as a native app on phones.

Why this scales

Web standards. Zero cost to deploy.

No app store. No custom OS. No proprietary hardware. Any device with a browser made in the last five years can run it. Any organization can self-host it on a $5 server, or a $60 Raspberry Pi at the school for true zero-internet operation.
